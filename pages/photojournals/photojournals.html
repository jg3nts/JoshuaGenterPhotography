Hokan-ji Temple

The construction of this pagoda was said to have been inspired by a dream of the Imperial Prince Shotoku in the year 589.

Kyoto City, Japan is home to the historic Hokan-ji Temple also known as Yosaka-no-to (Yasaka Pagoda). This temple attracts many tourists throughout the year ranging from school groups to wedding parties.

Fun fact: if you look closely at the corners of the temples' roof, each story is connected by a wire. this is to ground the structure as a form of lightning protection.

Sea Caves

Sea caves, also known as a littoral cave, is a type of cave which is primarily formed by the eroding forces of waves.

Sea caves are among the smallest of cave types. Factors which contribute to the formation of these caves including the nature of the zone, i.e tidal/swell conditions and the specific type of host rock.

Fun fact: Overtime, the ceiling of sea cave chambers may erode so much that they collapse forming a large littoral sinkhole.

Kings Guards

Organized by the British Army's Household Division, the King's Guards (or Queen's when the reigning Monarch is female) are sentry postings at Buckingham Palace and St. James's Palace.

The State Ceremonial Musicians of the Bands of the Household Division (seen in photo) has a long and prestigious history; te bands are an iconic part of British national heritage.

Fun fact: Drawn from the Royal Corps of Army Music, the musicians of the Household Division each serve in either one of 7 Regimental Bands: Grenadiers, Coldstream, Scots, Irish and Welsh Guards of the Foot Guards Bands, the Household Cavalry Band
